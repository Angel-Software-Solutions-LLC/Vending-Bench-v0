Display
A high-contrast serif drawn for editorial headlines and the quiet authority of a book cover. Six weights. Roman & italic.

Philosophy

Restraint over ornament. Classical proportion over novelty. The page is the reader's room — we furnish it carefully, then step out.

Kepler Display is shaped for the moment a page is first opened: the headline that holds, the title that earns its silence.

Reading Texture

The making of a typeface is, in the end, an act of trust extended to a stranger. The designer fixes proportion, rhythm, and weight; the reader, decades later and a continent away, asks only that the letters disappear into meaning. Kepler Display was drawn against that bargain. Its high contrast is tuned to the scale of a chapter opening or a magazine cover, where every curve is visible and every joint must answer for itself. Set smaller, the italic carries memory of a writing hand; set larger, the roman holds the page like a column holds a portico — present, structural, unhurried.
