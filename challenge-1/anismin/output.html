Philosophy

Restraint is a posture, not an absence. We draw letters that step back so the sentence can step forward.

Kepler Display is shaped by classical proportion — the wide capitals of inscriptional Roman, the modulation of the eighteenth-century didone, the patient hand of the punchcutter. It is built to serve the reader, never to perform for them.

Reading Texture · 10 / 16 pt

Johannes Kepler arrived in Linz with a satchel of unfinished proofs and a habit of writing in the margins. He believed the heavens were legible — that ellipses and harmonies were not impositions on the world but its native grammar. To set his name on a typeface is to make a small, deliberate claim: that letters, like orbits, can be governed by reason and warmed by intuition in the same breath.

Kepler Display is not a workhorse. It is a specimen for the moments when restraint becomes its own kind of emphasis — the title page, the chapter opening, the lone word above a photograph. Its high contrast asks for generous size and generous space; in return it offers a clarity that does not raise its voice.

We drew it slowly, in Vienna, between coffee and snowfall, and we hope it earns a small corner of your work.
